Why Study in Malaysia?

Malaysia offers a unique blend of Asian cultures and modern infrastructure, making it a vibrant and welcoming destination. It is a highly cost-effective option for students seeking an international education, with many reputable universities offering globally recognized programs in English and branch campuses of top UK and Australian universities.

Unique Advantages

  • Affordable Living: Enjoy a high standard of living at a significantly lower cost compared to many other countries.
  • Cultural Diversity: Experience a multicultural hub where Malay, Chinese, Indian, and indigenous cultures coexist harmoniously.
  • Transnational Education Hub: Study for a UK or Australian degree at a Malaysian branch campus for a fraction of the price.

Entry Requirements

Entry requirements are generally flexible and vary between public and private institutions.

  • Undergraduate: Completion of high school (A-Levels, IB, or equivalent). Some foundation or diploma pathways are available for students who don't meet direct entry requirements.
  • Postgraduate: A recognized bachelor's degree in a relevant field.
  • English Language: An IELTS score of 5.5 - 6.5 or a TOEFL equivalent is commonly required for most programs.

Living & Tuition Costs

Malaysia is one of the most affordable countries for international students.

  • Average Tuition Fees: $4,000 - $8,000 USD per year for Bachelor's degrees at private universities.
  • Estimated Living Costs: $400 - $700 USD per month, which covers accommodation, food, and other personal expenses.

Work Rights for Students

International students are allowed to work part-time for a maximum of 20 hours per week during semester breaks or holidays of more than 7 days. This helps students gain work experience and supplement their living expenses.

Scholarships

Many Malaysian universities offer scholarships and financial aid to deserving international students. Additionally, the Malaysian government provides opportunities like the Malaysia International Scholarship (MIS) for postgraduate studies.

Main University Intakes

Intake periods in Malaysia are frequent, offering great flexibility for students to begin their studies.

  • Primary Intakes: January, May, September.
  • Other Intakes: Many private universities also offer intakes in March, July, and October.

Malaysia, Affordable and Globally Recognized Education

Experience world-class scholarships, economic immersion, and cultural discovery—all in one transformative journey.

Why Study in Malaysia?

Malaysia offers advantages that are harder to find elsewhere — especially for students who value both quality and practicality.

Key Benefits of Studying in Malaysia

  • Dual Degrees from World-Class Universities
    Earn a degree from top UK or Australian universities like Monash or Nottingham at their Malaysian branch campus for almost 50% less cost.
  • Affordable World-Class Education
    With tuition fees between $4,000–$7,000/year and living costs around $400–$700/month, you can get an internationally respected degree with minimal debt.
  • English as a Main Language of Instruction
    Most professional programs are taught in English, removing language barriers while allowing you to learn strategic languages like Malay and Mandarin.
  • Multicultural Environment That Mirrors Global Workplaces
    The unique blend of Malay, Chinese, and Indian cultures provides an edge in cross-cultural communication skills essential for multinational corporations.
  • Strategic Location for Exploring Asia
    Located in the heart of Southeast Asia and a budget airline hub, you can easily explore Singapore, Thailand, or Vietnam for networking and cultural immersion.
  • Growing Opportunities in Fast-Developing Sectors
    Malaysia is investing heavily in tech, healthcare, and sustainable industries, offering internship and job opportunities in future-focused fields.

Work Rights for Students

International students in Malaysia are allowed to work part-time, but with certain conditions to ensure studies remain the priority.

  • Part-Time Work During Studies
    Students can work up to 20 hours per week during semester breaks or holidays of more than 7 days.
  • Work Permit Requirements
    Before starting, you must get approval from the Immigration Department of Malaysia through your university’s international office. Working without a permit is a violation of immigration law.
  • Post-Study Work Opportunities
    While Malaysia does not have an open post-study work visa, you can stay and work if you secure a full-time job offer. Your employer will then apply for an Employment Pass on your behalf.

Scholarships in Malaysia

Studying in Malaysia is already affordable, and numerous scholarships make it even more accessible. From government awards to university-specific funding, Malaysia is opening doors for talented students from all over the world.

Inquire About Scholarships

Main Scholarship Opportunities

  • Malaysian Government Scholarships (MIS)
    Supports outstanding international students pursuing postgraduate studies, often covering tuition and providing a living allowance.
  • University-Specific Scholarships
    Most Malaysian universities, like the University of Malaya and Monash University Malaysia, offer their own merit-based scholarships that can cover partial to full tuition.
  • Belt and Road Scholarship
    For students from Belt and Road Initiative countries, Malaysia participates in collaborative programs covering tuition and living expenses.

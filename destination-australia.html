Why Study in Australia?

Australia stands out as a leading destination for international students who seek exceptional post-study work rights, a globally recognized qualification system, and unmatched pathways to permanent residency (PR).

Key Benefits of Studying in Australia

  • Longest Post-Study Work Rights in the World
    Australia offers the most generous post-study work rights through the Temporary Graduate Visa (subclass 485), allowing graduates to work for up to six years—more than Canada, the UK, or the US for equivalent degrees. Since July 2023, graduates in critical sectors such as health, teaching, engineering, and ICT receive an extra two years on their work visa.
  • Australian Qualifications Framework (AQF)
    Global Recognition: Australia's AQF uniquely standardizes education levels across all institutions, from vocational training to PhDs. This ensures your degree is instantly recognized overseas and allows smooth transitions between fields and further study.
  • Flexible Work Options for Students
    Unlike other countries, Australia lets students work unlimited hours during official semester breaks. Those in the aged care sector currently enjoy no cap on work hours until at least mid-2025.
  • Regional Study
    More Immigration Points & Extended Visas: Studying in designated regional areas means lower tuition and living costs and up to 15 extra points toward PR under the General Skilled Migration points test. Regional graduates can also extend their post-study work visa by 1-2 years.
  • Study-Migration Alignment
    Choose In-Demand Occupations: Australia's Medium and Long-Term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL) and Regional Occupation List (ROL) directly link academic programs with migration needs, boosting chances of PR.
  • Unmatched Climate and Cultural Diversity
    Australia combines tropical, temperate, and arid climates in one country, giving students many lifestyle choices. With one of the highest international student ratios globally, Australia offers seamless cultural integration.

Entry Requirements

Understanding the entry requirements and application timeline is key to securing your future.

Bachelor's Degree (Undergraduate)

  • Academic Requirements: Completion of secondary/high school equivalent to Australian Year 12, with a minimum average of 65-75%+.
  • English Language Proficiency: IELTS Academic: 6.5 overall (no band below 6.0) or TOEFL iBT: 80-90 overall.

Master's Degree (Postgraduate)

  • Academic Requirements: A completed Bachelor's degree in a relevant field with a minimum GPA of 3.0/4.0 or equivalent.
  • Supporting Documents: Statement of Purpose (SOP), 2-3 letters of recommendation, and academic transcripts.
  • English Language Proficiency: IELTS Academic: 6.5-7.0 overall or TOEFL iBT: 90-100 overall.

PhD (Doctoral)

  • Academic Requirements: Completion of a Master's degree by research or an honors-level Bachelor's degree with strong academic performance.
  • Supporting Documents: Detailed research proposal, 3 strong recommendation letters, and academic transcripts.
  • English Language Proficiency: IELTS Academic: 6.5-7.0+ overall or TOEFL iBT: 90-100+ overall.

Work Rights for Students

International students in Australia enjoy generous work rights, supporting both financial stability and invaluable work experience:

  • During academic semesters: Students are permitted to work up to 40 hours per fortnight (every two weeks).
  • During official course breaks: Students can work unlimited hours, maximizing the opportunity to earn and learn.
  • Special provision for aged care: Those employed in the aged care sector have no cap on work hours until at least mid-2025.

Upon graduation, you may qualify for the Temporary Graduate Visa (subclass 485), granting you the chance to work and live in Australia for up to six years.

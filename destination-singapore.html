Study in Singapore

A global hub for education, technology, and innovation.

Why Study in Singapore?

Singapore is a vibrant, multicultural city-state renowned for its world-class education system, technological innovation, and safe, clean environment. It serves as a strategic gateway to Asia, offering unparalleled networking opportunities in business, finance, and technology.

Unique Advantages

  • Top-Ranked Universities: Home to institutions like NUS and NTU, which are consistently ranked among the best in the world.
  • Global Business Hub: Gain exposure to a thriving economy with countless multinational corporations and startups.
  • Safe and Efficient: Enjoy a high quality of life in one of the safest and most well-organized cities globally.

Entry Requirements

Admission to Singaporean universities is competitive and based on strong academic performance.

  • Undergraduate: High school qualifications such as A-Levels, IB Diploma, or a local Polytechnic Diploma are commonly accepted.
  • Postgraduate: A good bachelor's degree from a reputable university is required.
  • English Language: An IELTS score of 6.5 - 7.0 or a TOEFL equivalent is typically necessary for admission.

Living & Tuition Costs

While tuition is higher than in some Asian countries, it is competitive compared to the US and UK.

  • Average Tuition Fees: S$17,000 - S$35,000 per year for undergraduate programs at public universities.
  • Estimated Living Costs: Approximately S$1,000 - S$2,000 per month, covering accommodation, food, transport, and other expenses.

Work Rights for Students

International students attending approved institutions are permitted to work up to 16 hours per week during term time and full-time during vacation periods without needing a work permit.

Scholarships

The Singaporean government and universities offer various scholarships, such as the ASEAN Undergraduate Scholarship and the Singapore International Graduate Award (SINGA), to attract top international talent.

Main University Intakes

Most universities in Singapore have one main intake period per year.

  • Main Intake: Starts in August. The application period is typically from October to March.
  • Minor Intake: Some universities offer a smaller intake in January for select programs.
